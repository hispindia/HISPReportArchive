ANNUAL REQUEST TO WHO FOR ANTI-LEPROSY MEDICINE SUPPLY
 

Since 1991, The World Health Organization (WHO) and the pharmaceutical company Novartis International AG (Novartis) have been collaborating with the vision of a world free of leprosy. Both parties have concluded an agreement committing to donate medicines for the treatment of leprosy worldwide. As part of the global effort to fight against leprosy, WHO facilitates the supply of MDT medicines to treat leprosy patients and loose Clofazimine to manage ENL reactions. The medicines are delivered free of charge to countries following an official request through their Ministries of Health or those organisations working in association with or by written permission of a Ministry of Health.

This Excel-based tool is designed to assist countries in quantifying the number of relevant anti-leprosy medicines required to treat leprosy patients during the request/supply year in question. Output of the tool is a completed request for anti-leprosy medicines, which should be printed, signed and submitted to WHO.

Application Process and General Instructions:
  1. The deadline for application is from 1 July – 31 July of every year.
  2. Request/Supply year: 18-month period from July of the current year to December of the next year (for instance, 2021 supply year covers the period from July 2020 to December 2021). Here is the link to information about the MDT supply process.
  3. Mandatory data should be available before you start filling the form. Please be prepared to provide data as follows:

    MDT medicine: number of new cases detected in N-1 (where N is the year when the request was submitted), registered cases or cases under treatment and registered for further treatement on a most recent date available, estimation of the new case trend for the supply year in question, stocks of MDT medicines at 3 levels (central, regional, district), medicines expiring within 12 months after the stock-taking date (batch No, expiry month, quantity), expired medicines during N-1 (disaggregated by administrative levels), treatment protocol in place, requirement for MDT blisters.

    Clofazimine 50 mg and 100 mg: Cases with ENL reaction detected in N-3 and N-2 (where N is the request year), estimation of the ENL case trend for the supply year in question, registered cases or cases on treatment on a date (most recent data available), stocks of Clofazimine 50mg and 100mg at 3 levels (central, regional, district), Clofazimine stock movement report (total last year balance, total received from WHO between the past and current application, total medicines released from stock (distributed to sub-levels), total adjustments (expired, lost etc.), total balance on the most recent stock-taking date (or date of last stock check), Clofazimine stock expiring within 12 months after the stock-taking date (batch No, expiry month, quantity), treatment protocol in place, requirement for loose Clofazimine.

    Supply Information: Consignee and final recipient contact and address, notify parties (request-related and delivery-related), local import requirements (necessity of a green light, duration of a green light application, set of shipping documents). We recommend reaching your WHO leprosy focal point at your WHO country office first and discuss which party will be responsible for import and what import requirements are. If WHO is the consignee, you can ask your WHO focal point to assist in filling out the supply information section.

  4. To have the access to the request form, please read these instructions, enter the name of your country, select request year and medicine(s) to apply for and press the button "Start the request" located on the bottom of the page.
  5. Read instructions when selecting a data sell and comments when pointing section titles and cells in red and blue.
  6. Add information about planned detection activities and other important information in the sell dedicated to Section 16.
  7. To request medicines, the form should be submitted to WHO: printed out, signed and stamped, scanned and sent to the WHO contacts indicated in Section 18.

    !!!!! Please send 2 files to WHO: the signed copy in PDF format and this excel file !!!!!

  8. Make sure that before printing the form, you read WHO Data Sharing Policy in Section 16. If you accept it, please tick the correspondent checkbox. If not accepted, please leave the reason of not accepting the policy in Section 16.
  9. To print out the form, please create a PDF file by pressing the button "Save in PDF" located after Section 17. When the PDF file is open you can print it out and save in a file in PDF. To save the form in Excel please use the button "Save in Excel" located after Section 17. The file will be bearing the name of your country, request year and date when the document has been saved.
  10. In case additional replenishment is required after the submission of the request, additional supplies can be requested by completing the Additional Request Form. This form can be found on the MDT donation webpage (see the link below).

WHO Data Sharing Policy Statement

Data are the basis for all sound public health actions and the benefits of data sharing are widely recognized, including scientific and public health benefits. Whenever possible, WHO wishes to promote the sharing of health data, including but not restricted to surveillance and epidemiological data.

In this connection, and without prejudice to information sharing and publication pursuant to legally binding instruments, by providing data to WHO, the Ministry of Health of

confirms that all data to be supplied to WHO hereunder have been collected in accordance with applicable national laws, including data protection laws aimed at protecting the confidentiality of identifiable persons;

agrees that WHO shall be entitled, subject always to measures to ensure the ethical and secure use of the data, and subject always to an appropriate acknowledgement of

  1. to publish the data, stripped of any personal identifiers and make the Data available to any interested party on request (to the extent they have not, or not yet, been published by WHO) on terms that allow non-commercial, not-for-profit use of the Data for public health purposes (provided always that publication of the Data shall remain under the control of WHO);
  2. to use, compile, aggregate, evaluate and analyse the Data and publish and disseminate the results thereof in conjunction with WHOs work and in accordance with the Organizations policies and practices.

Except where data sharing and publication is required under legally binding instruments (IHR, WHO Nomenclature Regulations 1967, etc.), the Ministry of Health of may in respect of certain data opt out of (any part of) the above, by notifying WHO thereof in writing at the following address, provided that any such notification shall clearly identify the data in question and clearly indicate the scope of the opt-out (in reference to the above), and provided that specific reasons shall be given for the opt out.
